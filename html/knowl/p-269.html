This is an example of a recurrence relation. We represented one instance of our counting problem in terms of two simpler instances of the problem. It holds because the strings in \(\b B^5_3\) all have the form \(1\b B^4_2\) (that is, a 1 followed by a string from \(\b B^4_2\)) or \(0\b B^4_3\). If only we knew the cardinalities of \(\b B^4_2\) and \(\b B^4_3\). Repeating the same reasoning, \begin{equation*} |\b B^4_2| = |\b B^3_1| + |\b B^3_2| \quad \mbox{and} \quad |\b B^4_3| = |\b B^3_2| + |\b B^3_3|. \end{equation*}

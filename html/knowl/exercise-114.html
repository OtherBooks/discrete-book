Consider all functions \(f: \{1,2,3,4,5\} \to \{1,2,3,4,5\}\). How many of the injections have the property that \(f(x) \ne x\) for any \(x \in \{1,2,3,4,5\}\)?

Your friend claims that the answer is: \begin{equation*} 5! - \left[ {5\choose 1}4! - {5 \choose 2}3! + {5\choose 3}2! - {5 \choose 4}1! + {5\choose 5}0! \right]. \end{equation*}

Explain why this is correct.

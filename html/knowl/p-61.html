The other common operation on sets is intersection. We write, \begin{equation*} C = A \cap B \end{equation*} and say, “\(C\) is the intersection of \(A\) and \(B\),” when the elements in \(C\) are precisely those both in \(A\) and in \(B\). So if \(A = \{1, 2, 3\}\) and \(B = \{2, 3, 4\}\), then \(A \cap B = \{2, 3\}\).

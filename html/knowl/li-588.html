Item b
We must select 3 of the 11 dots. This can be done in \({11 \choose 3}\) ways. However, this will also give us degenerate triangles when all three vertices are on the \(x\)-axis or on the \(y\)-axis. There are \({7 \choose 3}\) ways we could have picked all three vertices on the \(x\)-axis. There are \({5 \choose 3}\) ways we could have picked all three vertices on the \(y\)-axis. Therefore the total number of triangles is \begin{equation*} {11 \choose 3} - {7 \choose 3} - {5 \choose 3} = 120 \end{equation*}

Example2.2.4

Let \(A = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}\), \(B = \{2, 4, 6\}\), \(C = \{1, 2, 3\}\) and \(D = \{7, 8, 9\}\). Determine which of the following are true, false, or meaningless.

  1. \(A \subset B\).
  2. \(B \subset A\).
  3. \(B \in C\).
  4. \(\emptyset \in A\).
  5. \(\emptyset \subset A\).
  6. \(A \lt D\).
  7. \(3 \in C\).
  8. \(3 \subset C\).
  9. \(\{3\} \subset C\).
